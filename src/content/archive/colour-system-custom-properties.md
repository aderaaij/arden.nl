Custom properties changed how colour systems could work in CSS: instead of compiling palettes into every selector with a preprocessor, you could define a small set of semantic tokens at the root and swap them at runtime.

This post covered splitting raw palette values from semantic roles, theming by re-assigning the semantic layer, and the fallback story for browsers that hadn't caught up yet.
